Ned LeDoux Bio-Wiki

Ned LeDoux is an American country music singer and songwriter who is the son of legendary country music singer-songwriter Chris LeDoux. Ned has released one studio album.

In 2015, Ned began recording solo material and working with producer Mac McAnally. In 2016, his debut EP Forever a Cowboy came out. On it, LeDoux wrote four of the five songs on it. Ned’s first full-length album then followed in 2017.

He has also appeared on Chase Rice’s 2017 album Lambs & Lions, as a duet partner on a rendition of Chris LeDoux’s “This Cowboy’s Hat”. LeDoux is set to release his new album  Next In Line on November 8.

Ned LeDoux Age

LeDoux was born on August 4, 1977, in Kaycee, Wyoming, U.S. He is 42 years old as of 2019.

Ned LeDoux Parents | Ned LeDoux Family

He was born to Chris LeDoux a legendary country music singer-songwriter, bronze sculptor, and hall of fame rodeo champion. His mother is Peggy Rhoads. LeDoux was born in a family of five children. His siblings are Clay, Will, Deau, and Cindy.

Ned LeDoux Father

Ledoux began playing in his father’s road band, Western Underground, in 1998. His father died after a battle with cancer in March 2005 at the age of 56.

However, Ned keeps his father’s legacy alive with a revamped lineup of Western Underground.

Ned LeDoux Wife

LeDoux has been married to his wife, Morgan for over a decade. The couple has two children together, a son Bronson, and a daughter Haven Jo.

Ned LeDoux Daughter’s Death

LeDoux’s 2-year-old daughter, Haven died on October 20th, 2019 due to a choking accident at the family home in Northeast Kansas. “Paramedics were called to the family’s northeast Kansas home on October 20th and arrived quickly, but attempts to resuscitate her were unsuccessful,” the statement reads.

“It is with great sadness that Ned & Morgan inform friends and fans that their two-year-old daughter Haven passed away on October 20th due to a tragic choking accident in the home,” the post continues. “The LeDoux family appreciates your love and support and is requesting privacy at this time.”

LeDoux had celebrated his daughter’s birthday in an Instagram post with a caption that read, “Turned the big #2 today! Happy to be home for this special day. What a character this one.”
