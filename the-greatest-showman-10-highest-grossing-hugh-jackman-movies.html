10 Highest Grossing Hugh Jackman Movies

Born and raised in Australia, Hugh Jackman began his career at five, starring as King Arthur in the Camelot production. After graduating college, he worked temporary jobs to pay for his classes. He honed his acting skills by studying at a Performing Arts Academy before making a television debut in Corelli in 1995. After working on several television shows, Jackman’s big Hollywood debut came with the 2000 movie X-Men where he starred as the anti-hero Wolverine. While working in the X-Men franchise, he also starred in romantic comedies and musicals, earning several accolades, including a Tony Award in 2004. As an established star in Broadway and Hollywood, Hugh Jackman has made millions working in blockbuster movies.

From playing the fan-favorite role of Wolverine to being a part of Oscar-nominated musical movies, Hugh Jackman has come a long way from his humble beginnings in Australia. Let’s take a look at some of his highest-grossing movies.

Real Steel (2011)

When action and science-fiction blend together, a blockbuster movie is born. Real Steel starring Hugh Jackman and Evangeline Lily, is based in a near-future setting where robot boxing is popular. The film was a commercial success and made $299 million at the office box. As mentioned by Go Banking Rates, Jackman was paid $9 million for his lead role in the movie.

Van Helsing (2004)

A complicated horror fiction about a monster-hunting vigilante Van Helsing was a different movie Hugh Jackman picked up after his success with the X-Men franchise, working with actress Kate Beckinsale. The actor earned $2 million for his starring role, while the movie made $300 million worldwide.

X2: X-Men United (2003)

In the anticipated sequel to the first X-Men movie, Jackman’s character of Wolverine was loved by all. He was paid $1 million to reprise the role. The plot saw Professor X kidnapped while the students at the school teamed up with Magneto to bring him back. The movie made a whopping $408 million, which indicated that it would be one of the most lucrative franchises for 20th Century Fox.

The Wolverine (2013)

A combination of action-packed and entertaining, The Wolverine was a standalone movie from the X-Men franchise that saw Jackman’s character going to Japan to meet an old friend. According to Celebrity Net Worth, Jackman was paid a whopping $20 million to put on the claws again, and the movie was a blockbuster at the box office, making $415 million worldwide.

The Greatest Showman (2017)

The first musical drama on the list is The Greatest Showman, also the movie with the greatest cast starring Jackman, Zendaya, Michelle Williams, and Zac Efron. Jackman played the Greatest Showman P. T. Barnum, who became a worldwide sensation. The magnetic cast, storytelling, and music made it a box office hit, earning $435 million. The actor was reportedly paid $10 million for his exuberant role.

Les Misérables (2012)

Adapted from the book by Victor Hugo, Les Misérables was a sensational tale about courage and love. Jackman played the lead role of Jean Valjean and earned $5 million in salary, as stated by Celeb Answers. The actor was nominated for a Golden Globe, an Academy Award, and SAG Award for his role. His performance received positive reviews, and the movie made $442 million.

X-Men: The Last Stand (2006)

Reprising the role of Logan or Wolverine, X-Men: The Last Stand was another movie in the successful franchise in which the government discovered a cure for mutation while Jean Gray has become the Dark Phoenix. The movie was well-received by audiences, making $459 million, and Jackman was paid $5 million to return to his iconic role.

X-Men: Apocalypse (2016)

X-Men: Apocalypse is the last movie from the collaborative franchise where Hugh Jackman strapped the claws. Set in the 1980s, it takes everyone back to when Logan was made into Wolverine. Jackman makes a cameo in the movie; however, he was paid millions for his minor role. The movie earned $544 million at the box office, making it an instant success.

Logan (2017)

Hugh Jackman’s final bow as the Wolverine, Logan, is a movie about sacrifice and making the right decision. Jackman finally hung his claws after reprising the role for the last time, and the film is said to be one of his best performances. An emotional goodbye to the character, Logan made $619 million at the box office, and Jackman earned millions of dollars from his role. Jackman had made over $100 million from the X-Men franchise altogether, as noted by The Things.

X-Men: Days Of Future Past (2014)

The most successful X-Men movie, Days Of Future Past, took a unique take with time travel for the first time in its franchise. Meeting young Professor X and Magneto was a delight on the screen, and Hugh Jackman as Wolverine was the cherry on top. The movie earned an astonishing $748 million at the box office, while Jackman was paid $7 million for his role.

Some of his other high-grossing movies include Australia, Swordfish, and Prisoners. From playing a gritty character as The Wolverine to singing in musicals that earned numerous nominations, Hugh Jackman’s dedication and determination led the young man in acting school to becoming a Hollywood star. The actor is set to show off his golden pipes on Broadway with the return of The Music Man in December 2021.
