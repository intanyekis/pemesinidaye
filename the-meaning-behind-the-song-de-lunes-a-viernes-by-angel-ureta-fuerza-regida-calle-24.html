The Meaning Behind The Song: De Lunes A Viernes by Angel Ureta, Fuerza Regida & Calle 24

De Lunes A Viernes is a popular song by Angel Ureta, Fuerza Regida, and Calle 24. Released on April 12, 2023, as part of the album “Otro Amanecer,” this song has gained significant attention in the Pop, En Español, Corrido, Latin Urban, and Regional Mexicano genres. Produced by JOP and written by Angel Ureta, JOP, and Diego Millán, this powerful track showcases the talent and storytelling abilities of these incredible artists.

Now let’s delve into the meaning behind the song, “De Lunes A Viernes.” This powerful track captures the struggles, resilience, and determination of the artists as they navigate through the hardships encountered in their daily lives.

The lyrics of the song highlight the dedication and hard work put in by the artists, particularly from Monday to Friday. The line “De lunes a viernes ando trabajando” (From Monday to Friday, I’m working) emphasizes their commitment to their craft and their relentless pursuit of success. The artists address those who question their achievements and speculate about their methods of reaching their goals, reminding them that it is their blessings from God and their steadfastness that have propelled them forward.

The track also touches upon their origins and the ongoing journey to overcome obstacles. They proudly proclaim their affiliation and loyalty to the same group of individuals who have been with them since the beginning. The line “Empecé tirando y ahora ando pegando con los mismos cholos que me acompañaron” (I started throwing and now I’m sticking with the same homeboys who supported me) reflects their unity and the camaraderie that fuels their success.

Additionally, the artists allude to their experiences on both sides of the border, referencing their enjoyment of life in the United States while asserting their Mexican heritage. The line “Si pregunta el migra, soy del otro lado” (If immigration asks, I’m from the other side) highlights their dual identity and the way they navigate their lives within the context of their cultural origins.

Throughout the song, Fuerza Regida, Calle 24, and Angel Ureta display their pride, resilience, and their willingness to take risks. They compare themselves to historical figures like Pancho Villa, demonstrating their determination to fight for what they believe in and their ability to navigate the challenges that come their way.

Personally, “De Lunes A Viernes” resonates with me because it tells a story of perseverance and the pursuit of dreams. The lyrics outline the hardships and sacrifices these artists have faced on their journey to success, serving as a reminder that hard work and dedication can lead to incredible achievements.
