YoungBoy Never Broke Again, also known as NBA YoungBoy, is a rapper who has gained popularity for his raw and authentic lyrics. His song “Drop’Em” is a track from his album Still Flexin, Still Reppin, released on March 10, 2020. The song was produced by NeilOnDaTrack and Guwap$ Of Beat Execz.

“Drop’Em” starts off with YoungBoy expressing his distrust in people and his intention to take legal action if anything goes wrong. The chorus talks about how he and his crew are ready to take down anyone who stands in their way. The lyrics depict a lifestyle filled with violence and the pursuit of power.

In the verses, YoungBoy raps about his wealth, expensive cars, and his confidence in his ability to handle any situation. He talks about the trials and tribulations he has faced, and how they have made him stronger. YoungBoy also mentions his association with dangerous individuals and the consequences that come with crossing them.

The song’s catchy chorus and hard-hitting verses are characteristic of YoungBoy’s style, which resonates with many listeners. The aggressive tone of the song reflects a street mentality that is prevalent in the rap genre.

Personally, “Drop’Em” is a song that I find myself drawn to because of its energy and intensity. The lyrics may be dark and intense, but they create a sense of power and determination. When I listen to this song, it serves as a reminder that I have the strength to overcome any obstacles in my path.

As with many of YoungBoy’s songs, “Drop’Em” can be open to interpretation. While some may view it as promoting violence or a negative lifestyle, others may see it as a reflection of the artist’s experiences and a way for him to express himself. It’s important to approach the song with an open mind and recognize that music is often a personal expression of an artist’s emotions and experiences.

In conclusion, “Drop’Em” by YoungBoy Never Broke Again is a rap song that showcases the artist’s raw and authentic style. The lyrics depict a lifestyle filled with violence and the pursuit of power. However, it’s important to recognize that music is subjective, and interpretations may vary. For me personally, this song is empowering and serves as a reminder of my own strength.
